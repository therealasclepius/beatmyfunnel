# Beat My Funnel — Pricing Strategy

## Two Sides to Price
- **Brands** — posting challenges
- **Operators** — entering challenges

---

## Core Model: Platform Fee on Prize Pool

Take **15-20%** of every prize paid out.

**Example flow:**
Brand posts a $10,000 challenge → someone wins → brand pays $10,000 + 15% platform fee ($1,500) → operator receives the full $10,000.

### Why this works:
- Zero friction to post (no upfront cost)
- You only make money when value is delivered
- Aligns incentives — you want challenges to be won
- Same model as 99designs, Upwork, most marketplaces

---

## What You Don't Charge For (Yet)

- **Posting a challenge** — free. Any friction here kills supply.
- **Entering a challenge** — free. Any friction here kills demand.
- **Challenges where nobody wins** — brand gets full refund, you take nothing. Keeps trust high.

---

## Layer 2 (Once You Have Traction)

**Featured listings** — brands pay $99-299 to pin their challenge at the top. Pure margin, no risk.

**Verified operator badge** — operators pay $29/mo to get a "verified" badge showing their win rate and past results. Builds the reputation layer, creates recurring revenue.

**Challenge spec review** — brand pays $199 for you to professionally define their win condition before posting. Reduces disputes, increases quality. High margin service.

---

## Unit Economics

Average challenge size: **$5,000-$25,000**

At 15% take rate:
- 1 challenge/week at $10k average = $1,500/week = **$78k/year**
- 10 challenges running concurrently = **$780k/year**

---

## Key Decision: Fee Structure

**Decision:** Charge the fee ON TOP of the prize, not out of it.

Operators should always receive the full advertised prize — that's the trust signal that attracts the best talent.

- Brand pays: $10k prize + $1,500 fee = $11,500 total
- Operator receives: $10,000 (full prize, untouched)
- Platform keeps: $1,500

**Start at 15%.** You can always lower it. Hard to raise it once people expect a number.
